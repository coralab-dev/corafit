# CoraFit Logo Design

## Goal

Create one original logo concept for CoraFit, a training platform that connects coaches with their clients. The identity should balance human support with energy, discipline, and visible progress.

## Direction: Impulso C

The logo mark uses two rounded, complementary strokes to form an open letter C. The outer gesture feels protective and approachable, while the negative space and forward-leaning inner gesture suggest upward momentum. The symbol must remain abstract: it should not depict a dumbbell, a literal person, or an obvious heart.

The primary lockup places the mark beside the exact wordmark `CoraFit`. The wordmark should feel geometric, modern, and friendly, with enough weight and spacing to remain legible at interface sizes.

## Visual System

- Primary coral: `#FE3D1B`
- Supporting colors: black and white
- Style: flat, minimal, vector-friendly, with a strong silhouette and balanced negative space
- Composition: centered horizontal lockup on a plain white background with generous padding
- Avoid: gradients, shadows, 3D effects, mockups, mascots, generic fitness imagery, extra text, and watermarks

## First Deliverable

Generate one polished raster concept board containing the primary symbol and the horizontal `CoraFit` lockup. This first output is for visual evaluation; it does not replace a production vector master.

Use the built-in image generation path with the `logo-brand` use case. Keep the generated image in the project under `output/imagegen/` so it can be reviewed and iterated without changing application code.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The text reads exactly `CoraFit` with no extra characters.
- The mark reads as an original abstract C at small size.
- The design communicates both support and forward progress.
- The coral, black, and white palette is consistent with the current product UI.
- The result contains no literal gym equipment, human silhouette, obvious heart, gradient, shadow, 3D treatment, mockup scene, trademark, or watermark.
- The composition is simple enough to recreate as a production vector after approval.
